Analysis

Engineering problems that cannot be solved using analytical methods are generally solved using numerical approximation. Numerical analysis solves partial differential equations of continuous type numerically (by discretizing) using different types of algorithms. In mechanical engineering this type of analysis are performed for structures, fluid flow and heat transfer using the following methods.

CFD

Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) is a numerical solving technique, with the help of computer languages and/or solvers, produces quantitative predictions of fluid-flow phenomena based on the conservation laws governing the fluid motion (Conservation mass, momentum, energy and species). Applied mainly for cases where analytical solutions cannot be used. Broadly classified as,

  • Finite Difference Method (FDM)
  • Finite Volume Method (FVM)

FEA

Finite Element analysis (FEA) is the simulation of the behaviour of a part or assembly of parts, to be evaluated with the finite element approach (FEM). Engineers utilize the FEA to model and simulate parts virtually before fabrication and therefore minimize physical prototype requirements, while also allowing the optimization of components as part of the project design process.
